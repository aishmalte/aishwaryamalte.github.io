Haldi Ceremony

Dress code: Yellow & Off-White ✨

A joyful pre-wedding ritual filled with turmeric, laughter, and blessings.

The Haldi Ceremony is a cherished pre-wedding ritual in Indian weddings. “Haldi” literally translates to turmeric — the star of the day — and sets the tone for a celebration of warmth, laughter, and blessings. The dress code is traditionally yellow, matching the vibrance of the ritual itself.

Turmeric holds great auspicious and cultural significance in Indian traditions. It symbolizes purity, prosperity, and good health. For centuries, it has been valued for its medicinal and beautifying properties, acting as a natural antiseptic that leaves the skin glowing — a fitting ritual before the wedding day.

The paste is typically made using turmeric, rose water, chickpea flour, and sometimes milk or yogurt — all natural ingredients symbolizing purity and simplicity. No cosmetics, only blessings!

The Joyful Ritual

During the ceremony, family members and friends apply the haldi paste to the bride and groom, offering blessings and good wishes for a prosperous and happy married life. The atmosphere is filled with laughter, music, and joy as everyone joins in the fun.

As the ceremony unfolds, it often takes a playful turn — guests shower the couple with flower petals, and sometimes, in the end, with a splash of water! The ritual signifies cleansing, renewal, and preparation for the sacred wedding ahead.

Tip: Expect to get a little messy — but that’s the fun of it! The Haldi ceremony is all about celebration, connection, and radiant beginnings.
